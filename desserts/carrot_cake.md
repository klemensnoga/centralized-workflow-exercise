# Carrot Cake

Yield: 9–12 servings  
Prep: 20 minutes · Cook: 40–45 minutes · Cool: 20 minutes

## Ingredients
- 2 cups (240 g) all-purpose flour  
- 1 1/2 tsp baking powder  
- 1 tsp baking soda  
- 1/2 tsp salt  
- 2 tsp ground cinnamon  
- 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg (optional)  
- 1 1/4 cups (250 g) granulated sugar  
- 1/2 cup (100 g) light brown sugar, packed  
- 3 large eggs, room temperature  
- 1 cup (240 ml) vegetable oil (or neutral oil)  
- 2 tsp vanilla extract  
- 2 cups (about 240 g) grated carrots (firmly packed)  
- 1/2 cup (60 g) chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)  
- 1/2 cup (75 g) raisins or crushed pineapple (optional)

## Cream Cheese Frosting
- 8 oz (225 g) cream cheese, softened  
- 1/4 cup (56 g) unsalted butter, softened  
- 2 cups (240–260 g) powdered sugar, sifted  
- 1 tsp vanilla extract  
- Pinch of salt

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9×13-inch pan or two 8-inch round pans; line with parchment if desired.  
2. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.  
3. In a large bowl, beat sugars, eggs, oil, and vanilla until combined. Gradually stir the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until just combined.  
4. Fold in grated carrots and any optional add-ins (nuts, raisins, or pineapple). Do not overmix.  
5. Pour batter into prepared pan(s) and smooth the top.  
6. Bake: 9×13 pan — 40–45 minutes; 8-inch rounds — 28–35 minutes. Cake is done when a toothpick inserted near the center comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs.  
7. Cool in the pan for 15–20 minutes, then remove to a rack to cool completely before frosting.

## Frosting
1. Beat cream cheese and butter until smooth and creamy.  
2. Add powdered sugar gradually, then vanilla and a pinch of salt. Beat until smooth and spreadable. Adjust texture with a little milk (if too thick) or more powdered sugar (if too thin).  
3. Frost cooled cake. Chill briefly to set if desired.

## Notes & Tips
- Use freshly grated carrots (not pre-shredded) for best texture.  
- For a lighter cake, swap 1/4 cup oil for applesauce.  
- Toast nuts briefly to bring out flavor.  
- Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days (frosting contains dairy). Freeze unfrosted layers up to 3 months.

## Variations
- Spiced: increase cinnamon to 3 tsp and add 1/2 tsp ground ginger.  
- Tropical: fold in 1/2 cup drained crushed pineapple and omit raisins.  
- Vegan: use flax eggs, plant-based cream cheese, and adjust baking time as needed.

Enjoy.
